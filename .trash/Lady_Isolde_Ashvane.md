# Lady Isolde Ashvane
*Noble — Hugo's Pursuer*

## Appearance
Tall, composed human woman, late 20s. Chestnut hair pinned with a silver comb. Pale green gown that cost more than the party's townhouse. Moves through a room like someone who has been trained since childhood to enter spaces with purpose. Meets eye contact directly and holds it a beat longer than comfortable.

## Background
Third daughter of House Ashvane — a minor but deeply ambitious noble family with significant mercantile holdings and an unfortunate shortage of political connections at the higher levels of Valandir society. Isolde has identified [[Hugo de Léon]] as a candidate. She has done her research thoroughly.

**What she knows about Hugo:**
- Born during a magical surge — significant political symbolism
- Heir to the de Léon name and its considerable weight
- Currently unattached
- Recently celebrated as a hero of Valandir
- Has a reputation for causing significant property damage

The last point is a concern. She has decided it is not disqualifying.

## Personality
- Precise and direct — she finds coyness inefficient
- Genuinely intelligent, not just socially adept
- Finds the party's chaos mildly alarming but secretly interesting
- Is not performing warmth — she is actually warm, beneath the calculation
- Has a dry sense of humour she deploys carefully

## The Hector Complication
[[Hector de Léon]] knows House Ashvane. He approves of the match — the family's ambition is manageable, their holdings useful, and Isolde specifically has the kind of composure he respects. He has said absolutely nothing to Hugo about this. He is watching from across the room with quiet satisfaction as she approaches.

This is the most paternal thing Hector has done all evening. Hugo will not see it that way.

## Purpose at the Gala
Social chaos for Hugo. She is not a villain or a threat — she is entirely rational about what she wants and sees no reason to pretend otherwise. She creates a situation Hugo must navigate in real time while also processing his conversation with his father.

Optional complication: she is actually good company. If Hugo's player engages genuinely rather than deflecting, she becomes an interesting recurring character with her own perspective on Valandir's politics.

## Key Lines
First approach:
> *"Hugo de Léon. I've heard a great deal about you."* A pause. *"Most of it alarming. I find I'm not deterred."*

If asked why she's interested:
> *"Your family's name carries weight. Your recent actions suggest you're not merely trading on it. That combination is rarer than you'd think."*

If Hugo deflects by mentioning he's leaving the city:
> *"How long?"* A beat. *"People who leave Valandir interestingly tend to return more interestingly. I'm patient."*

On the party's reputation:
> *"You broke out of the Adventurers' Guild, incited a public crowd, and apparently presented a severed head to a mob boss."* She considers this. *"My family has done worse for less."*

## What She Knows
- Standard Valandir political gossip — who owes what to whom
- That Helios is making moves against the party — she finds this worth mentioning if Hugo seems genuinely interested in the conversation
- Nothing about the expedition or the Hearthstone

## Connections
- [[Hugo de Léon]] — target of interest
- [[Hector de Léon]] — knows and approves of her family
- House Ashvane — her family, ambitious, mercantile
