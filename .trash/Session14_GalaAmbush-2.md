# Session 14 — The Gala Ambush

## The Setup
The party exit the gala to find their carriage on fire. The driver is on the ground. The weapons trunk has been unclipped from the carriage and sits nearby — close enough to the fire that in **2 turns it will also catch**. Anyone moving close enough to the trunk takes **fire damage**.

Hugo's plate armour is in the burning carriage — likely destroyed. The weapons trunk is the priority.

Six BCS men are waiting. Fully armed and armoured.

**Note:** Library guards are inside with Vaelis — Helios timed this deliberately. The party are alone outside.

**Friendly assets:** Two noble house guards from Hugo's rallied families are milling near the carriages. Untrained but present. Hugo can bring them in with a rally action.

---

## The Bottleneck — Why They Can't Just Run

The environment does the work:

- **Carriage corridor** — carriages line both sides of the street creating a natural funnel. Loom plants in the gap between two carriages. It's a real chokepoint, not just a man standing in a field.
- **Rook and Vetch seal the rear** — as the party exit the doors, these two circle behind them. The party are caught between the gala entrance and Loom ahead. It's a kill box.
- **Spooked horses** — the fire has panicked the carriage horses. The flanks are difficult terrain — bucking horses, tangled reins. Anyone trying to go around the wall makes a **Dexterity check** or takes damage and falls prone.

---

## The Trunk — 2 Turn Clock
- **Turn 1:** Trunk is hot. Fire damage to reach it.
- **Turn 2:** Trunk catches. Contents may be damaged or destroyed.
- **Crane** (archer) is positioned near the trunk — he is the obstacle between the party and their weapons.

## Party Options

### Option 1 — Fight Unarmed
Go straight at the BCS men. Buys time for others to reach the trunk. Magic users and high Strength characters fare best. Everyone else is at a disadvantage in gala clothes against armed thugs.
- TW can cast *Shillelagh* — no trunk needed
- Mog and Mimbi have magic — trunk less critical for them
- Hugo, Danerious and Gramdrom need those weapons

### Option 2 — Sprint for the Trunk
Someone fast goes straight for the trunk. Must get past or around **Crane** and **Loom**. Takes fire damage on arrival. Two turn window.
- High priority for martial characters
- Crane will use his reaction to shoot anyone making a dash

### Option 3 — Rally the Crowd
Hugo just scored Persuasion 19 inside. Noble families and their retinues are walking out those doors right now. A shout from Hugo naming Helios's men in public could turn this into a witnessed incident the BCS can't walk back.
- Could bring armed noble guards into the fight
- Hugo is the face — this costs him his first action

### The Smart Split
- **Hugo** — rally the nobles spilling out behind the party
- **Someone fast** (Vetch, Mog, Mimbi) — sprint for the trunk
- **Everyone else** — stall the BCS men, don't let Loom set the wall

---

## Tactical Watch List
- **Crane** must be dealt with early — he controls access to the trunk and will punish anyone sprinting for it
- **Loom** will try to create a chokepoint — area of effect spells break this
- **Patch** will go straight for Mimbi or any exposed caster — needs protecting
- **Rook** is dangerous precisely because he waits — he'll exploit whatever gap appears turn 1
- **Sable** is slow — kite him, don't stand still
