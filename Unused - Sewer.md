# The Valandir Sewers — Dungeon Crawl
## Route: Sewer Entrance → Warehouse Basement

*The party descend into the sewer system to avoid Helios's men on the surface. The route runs beneath the dockside district and surfaces directly into the basement of Helios's warehouse. Four encounters stand between them and Miss Jaunty.*

---

## Global Sewer Rules
These conditions apply in every room unless stated otherwise:

| Condition | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| **Single file movement** | No flanking formations. Frontliners cannot protect casters behind them. |
| **Low ceilings (6ft)** | No flying. Disadvantage on ranged attacks beyond 20ft. Large creatures are squeezed — disadvantage on attack rolls. |
| **Ankle to knee deep water** | Difficult terrain throughout all passages. |
| **Concentration checks** | Any hit forces a DC 10 Con save to maintain concentration. |
| **Darkness** | Torches or darkvision only. Extinguishing a light source is a viable tactic for enemies. |

## Room 1 — The Junction
*A branching junction where two passages meet. Brick archways, green slick walls, torchlight barely reaching the far end.*

### The Obstacle
A **Giant Crocodile** is wedged across the main passage, completely blocking it. It is not aggressive — it is eating something the party cannot identify. It simply fills the corridor.

There is a side passage to the left. It looks like a way around. It is not — it loops back and deposits the party into Room 2 from a different angle, having wasted time and movement.

### Encounter
**Giant Crocodile** — AC 12 | HP 114 | Speed 20ft (swim 30ft)

| Attack | Bonus | Damage | Effect |
|--------|-------|--------|--------|
| Bite | +8 | 3d10+5 piercing | Target grappled (escape DC 16). While grappled, target is restrained. |
| Tail | +8 | 2d8+5 bludgeoning | Target knocked prone. |

**Multiattack:** Bite + Tail each turn.

**Sewer tactics:** The crocodile fills a 5ft corridor completely. The party cannot get around it — they must fight through it. Anyone grappled is restrained in ankle deep water while the rest of the party are single file behind. Whoever is at the front is on their own until the croc is dead or driven back.

### DM Notes
- The croc has no interest in the party initially — first round it continues eating. It attacks on round 2 or if attacked first.
- Its tail can reach the second person in line.
- A successful DC 14 Animal Handling check calms it enough to squeeze past — risky, but possible.
- **Purpose of this room:** Establish the rules. Burn a few hit points. Show the party that single file changes everything.

## Room 2 — The Cistern Pillars
*A vaulted brick chamber, wider than the tunnels. Eight stone pillars rise from knee-deep water to a ceiling thick with moisture and shadow. Clicking sounds echo before anything is visible.*

### The Obstacle
**Six Giant Centipedes** cling to the pillars and ceiling. They drop in waves — three on round 1, three more on round 2 — staggered across the chamber so the party cannot AOE them all at once.

### Encounter
**Giant Centipede (x6)** — AC 13 | HP 4 each | Speed 30ft (climb 30ft)

| Attack | Bonus | Damage | Effect |
|--------|-------|--------|--------|
| Bite | +4 | 1d6+2 piercing + 3d6 poison | DC 11 Con save or poisoned until end of next turn |

**Sewer tactics:** Each centipede is trivial alone. The danger is the poisoned condition stacking across multiple party members — poisoned means disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks. In a confined space with limited AOE, these chip away at everyone.

Wave 1 (Round 1): Three drop from the ceiling at the front, middle and rear of the party simultaneously.
Wave 2 (Round 2): Three more drop from the pillars as the party deals with the first wave.

### DM Notes
- The pillars create partial cover (+2 AC) for centipedes clinging to them.
- Water makes fire-based AOE risky — any fire spell in knee-deep water hits the caster's feet too (1d6 fire damage, no save, flavour ruling).
- **Purpose of this room:** Poison stacking. Attrition begins. Spell slots start being spent on what should be trivial enemies.
- A party member who was already weakened by the croc fight starts to feel it here.

## Room 3 — The Nest Chamber
*The passage opens into a wide circular cistern. The ceiling rises to 12ft here — the first real breathing room. But the floor is chaos: debris, bones, rotting crates, and something that smells indescribable have been dragged into piles around a central mass. The water here is darker. Thicker.*

### The Obstacle
An **Otyugh** has lived here for years. It is intelligent enough — barely — to have arranged the debris around its nest as difficult terrain. It has also learned a few words of Common from listening to workers in the tunnels above.

It will not let the party pass without tribute or a fight.

**Opening line:** *"Meat. Give meat. Or you are meat."*

### Encounter
**Otyugh** — AC 14 | HP 114 | Speed 30ft

| Attack | Bonus | Damage | Effect |
|--------|-------|--------|--------|
| Tentacle (x2) | +6 | 2d6+3 bludgeoning | Target grappled (escape DC 13) and restrained |
| Tentacle Slam | +6 | 2d6+3 bludgeoning | Only usable on restrained target. DC 13 Con save or stunned until end of next turn |
| Bite | +6 | 2d8+3 piercing | DC 15 Con save or diseased — while diseased, target can't regain HP and has disadvantage on all ability checks and attack rolls. Lasts until cured. |

**Multiattack:** Two tentacle attacks OR one tentacle slam + bite.

**Sewer tactics:** The Otyugh controls its nest. Difficult terrain slows the party approaching it. Its tentacles reach 10ft — it can grab two people simultaneously and drag them into bite range. The disease from its bite is the real threat — a diseased party member is a serious liability in the warehouse fight to come.

### Negotiat toion
The Otyugh can be bribed. It wants meat — large and fresh. Options:
- Offer it the Giant Crocodile carcass from Room 1 (if someone had the foresight)
- A DC 12 Persuasion check with any food offering
- A DC 15 Persuasion check with nothing to offer — it's hungry but it can be reasoned with

A successful negotiation means it steps aside and watches them pass. It does not help. It does not follow. It just watches.

### DM Notes
- **Purpose of this room:** Decision point. Fight or negotiate? Smart parties save resources here. Proud parties spend them.
- If the party fight, the Otyugh fights to 20HP then retreats to its nest and stops pursuing — it's not suicidal.
- The disease from the bite is the lasting consequence. Someone being diseased walking into the warehouse changes the fight significantly.

## Room 4 — The Final Chamber
*The last chamber before the ladder up. The ceiling rises slightly — 8ft. The walls are newer brick, dockside construction. The ladder is visible at the far end, iron rungs set into the wall, leading up to a wooden trapdoor.*

*Standing between the party and the ladder is something that shouldn't fit in a sewer. And yet.*

### The Obstacle
A **Troll** — massive, knuckles dragging in the water. Crude BCS markings have been scratched into its grey-green skin. Helios put it here deliberately. This is not random. He knew someone might come this way.

It fills the corridor. It is waiting.

### Encounter
**Troll** — AC 15 | HP 84 | Speed 30ft

| Attack | Bonus | Damage | Effect |
|--------|-------|--------|--------|
| Bite | +7 | 1d6+4 piercing | — |
| Claw (x2) | +7 | 2d6+4 slashing | — |

**Multiattack:** Bite + two claws.

**Regeneration:** Regains 10HP at the start of each turn. Stopped only by fire or acid damage dealt since last turn.

**Sewer tactics:** The troll fills a 10ft wide corridor. It has reach — it can hit the second person in line with its claws. Its regeneration is the critical problem: fire and acid stop it, but both are dangerous in a confined sewer with the party in single file.

Fire spells in the confined space risk the party. Acid splash is precise but low damage. The party need to coordinate — someone deals fire/acid while others keep pressure on.

If the troll drops to 0HP without fire or acid, it rises again on its next turn with 10HP.

### DM Notes
- **Purpose of this room:** The gauntlet. The party arrive here drained — hit points low, spell slots spent, possibly poisoned or diseased. This is the hardest fight precisely because of what came before it.
- The troll knows the ladder is behind it. It will not retreat — Helios's men have made it clear what happens if it fails.
- If the party are in serious trouble, the trapdoor above the ladder rattles — someone in the warehouse basement heard the noise. Adds time pressure.
- Killing the troll without fire or acid is possible — it takes sustained damage across multiple turns, requiring the party to burn it down faster than it regenerates. At full efficiency a Troll regenerates 10HP per round — the party need to deal more than 10HP net damage per round to make progress.

### Reward
The ladder. The trapdoor. The warehouse basement.

## Surfacing — The Warehouse Basement
*The trapdoor opens into a dark stone basement. Crates. Rope. The smell of the river. A single torch on the wall.*

*And a door at the far end, bolted from this side, with light visible underneath it.*

*Behind that door — Miss Jaunty. And above, the sound of voices. Hugo's voice. And Helios.*

The water team has arrived. The clock starts now.
