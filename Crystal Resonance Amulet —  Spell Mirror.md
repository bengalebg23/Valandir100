

**Type:** Amulet (Wondrous Item)
**Rarity:** Very Rare
**Requires Attunement:** Yes (by a spellcaster)

---

### Properties

**Spell Mirror — Once per long rest**

When Mimbi sees a spell being cast within **60ft** — targeting her, an ally, or an area — she may use her **reaction** to attempt to absorb it into the crystal.

**The Contest:**
- Mimbi makes a **Charisma check**
- Contested against the caster's **spellcasting ability check**
- **On success:** The spell is absorbed and negated entirely. Nobody takes the effect. Mimbi may recast the same spell at any point before her next long rest by expending a **spell slot of equal or higher level**. She uses her own spell save DC and spell attack bonus when recasting.
- **On failure:** The spell resolves normally against its original target. The crystal goes dark and this property cannot be used again until the next dawn.

**Restrictions:**
- Once per long rest
- Costs her reaction — cannot be used if her reaction has already been spent this round
- She must expend her own spell slot to recast — the absorbed spell is not free
- The absorbed spell must be recast before her next long rest or it is lost
- Cannot absorb spells above **5th level**
- Cannot absorb cantrips

---

### Why It's Balanced
- Once per long rest
- Costs her reaction
- She must spend her own spell slot to fire it back — absorbing a 4th level spell costs a 4th level slot
- The contest is real — against a high level caster she may lose
- Failure is worse than doing nothing — the spell lands AND the amulet goes dark
- The decision of whether to spend the slot to recast is genuinely interesting every time

---

### Example Uses
- Absorb **Hypnotic Pattern** aimed at the party — negate it, recast it at Soren's mages using a 3rd level slot
- Absorb **Hold Person** targeting Hugo — negate it, recast it later at a single high-value target
- Absorb **Counterspell** aimed at Mimbi's own spell — negate it, potentially recast it to shut down an enemy spell later
- Absorb **Fireball** — negate 8d6 damage to the party, recast it at the enemy cluster using a 3rd level slot

### Description
A silver chain holding an oval setting of dark metal. Inside sits a shard of ley line crystal — pale gold-green, faintly warm. Found in Helios's private rooms, wrapped in cloth, separated from the main crystal shipment for reasons he never explained.

When Mimbi first touched it the crystal pulsed once. It has not done that for anyone else.

Near ley line convergence points the crystal brightens visibly. At the Reach and the Watchtower it was almost too warm to touch before they even arrived.

---

### Narrative Note
At the DM's discretion the crystal may interact with the Verdant Wrath at the portal. Suggested: the amulet provides **advantage on one** of Mimbi's five required Charisma checks to calm the Wrath, as the crystal recognises the energy of the ley lines it came from.

> *Spell Mirror (1/Long Rest): When you see a spell of 1st–5th level being cast within 60 feet, you may use your reaction to attempt to absorb it. Make a Charisma check contested by the caster's spellcasting ability check. On a success the spell is negated and absorbed — you may recast the same spell before your next long rest by expending a spell slot of equal or higher level, using your own spell save DC. On a failure the spell resolves normally and this property cannot be used until the next dawn.*

### Notes
- Best used against high-impact spells targeting the whole party — Hypnotic Pattern, Slow, Fear, Fireball
- Mimbi's player should track the absorbed spell and its level between sessions if not used immediately
- The recast window is generous — absorbed before a short rest can still be used in the next encounter
- Mirroring an enemy's own Hypnotic Pattern back at them is the intended power fantasy
