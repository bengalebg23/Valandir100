## Scene 2 — The Second Warehouse
Party arrive to find the crystal crate partially loaded onto the barge — dockhands working, dawn just breaking.
Soren's men are here — not knights, not BCS. Velmoran mages. Three or four of them overseeing the loading. They don't want a fight but they have orders.
This is a shorter, scrappier fight than the warehouse. Open dockside, moving crate, barge that could cast off. The objective isn't just defeat the mages — it's stop the barge leaving with any crystals on it.
One mage may Misty Step onto the barge and try to push off. Clock ticking.

## Scene 3 — The Library
Crystals delivered to Thalira and Kermin. Thalira begins coordinating distribution. She identifies two active convergence points the party should handle personally — The Reach first, then the old watchtower.
She also notes something quietly to Mimbi: "The closer you get to a convergence point the more you'll feel it. Your connection to the Feywild makes you sensitive to this. That's useful. But be careful — the energy doesn't know the difference between a conduit and a vessel."
That's a gentle warning for what's coming at the watchtower.

## Scene 4 — The Reach
Outdoors, city outskirts. Warm ground, charged air.
Mimbi places one crystal — the magic here is strong, responsive. It snaps into place almost eagerly.
The surge: Mimbi feels it immediately — a rush of Feywild energy, and for a few seconds a vision of her hometown. Colour. Movement. Life accelerating, then settling. It's working.
Then the ground ruptures. Two or three Ankhegs erupt — drawn by the magical discharge. Manageable fight, dramatic moment. The party realise that placing crystals has physical consequences on the world around them.
## Scene 5 — The Watchtower
The party have been here before — familiar ground but changed. The magic here is older, murkier. Thalira said it would need more crystals to shift.
They load five or six crystals into the convergence point. It takes longer. The energy builds gradually rather than snapping. Mimbi feels the surge more intensely this time — the vision of her hometown is longer, more vivid, something almost knocking against the boundary between worlds.
Then Soren arrives. Not alone — a small group of mages. He's been tracking them. He saw what happened at The Reach. He knows what they're doing and he's furious.
Before anyone can act — the ground tears open. The larger surge has pulled something through. The Otyugh from the sewer — two legs, three tentacles, drawn upward by the magical discharge, bigger and angrier than it was underground.
Three-way chaos:
Party vs the creature
Soren's mages vs the creature
Soren vs the party
Nobody planned this. Soren may have to make a choice — fight the party or fight the thing his crystal programme just pulled out of the ground. That's his first crack of doubt.
## Scene 6 — The Portal
Mimbi's hometown. The portal is already straining — something has been pressing against it from the Feywild side for a long time.
The party arrive. Soren is close behind — he understands now what the portal means. This is the final placement. If the party push enough crystals through here the whole ley line network restores and his programme is finished permanently.
He makes one last attempt to talk. Mimbi's argument — the crystals don't store the energy, they ARE the conduit — lands here. He's seen the Otyugh. He's seen the rift at the watchtower. His gauntlet has been flickering.
He doesn't stand down. But he hesitates.
The party push the crystals through.
The Verdant Wrath erupts.
Soren's hesitation becomes irrelevant — everything is chaos. The Wrath doesn't distinguish between anyone. Soren is thrown aside in the first surge. Helios is already dead.
The party's job: Protect Mimbi while she channels. Five successful DC 15 Charisma checks, one per round. The Wrath lashes out at everything. Survive long enough for her to calm it.
Five successes. The Wrath stills. The portal stabilises. The Feywild breathes.
Mimbi's hometown — visible through the portal for just a moment before it closes. Colour. Life. Home.
