[[Act 14 - The Gala]]

**Opening:** The wait at the Library. Three conversations happen before Helios responds.
**Middle:** Helios draws them to the warehouse. The party choose their approach.
**Climax:** The warehouse — split party, two objectives, one escape route.
**End:** Jaunty freed, crystals secured or lost, Helios escapes or is dealt with.

## Part 1 — The Wait at the Library

The party are outside the Library, post-carriage fight. Vaelis is bound. The raven has been sent. Now they wait.

---

### Scene A — Mimbi and Thalira Barkwood

Kermin quietly takes Mimbi aside and introduces her to **Thalira Barkwood** — senior Library researcher, adventurer, tasked with solving the magic distribution problem. Aurora's mother. She has been working on this longer than anyone.

**What Thalira tells Mimbi:**

The crystals were not created. They were *displaced* — torn from natural ley line convergence points across the region and concentrated underground by Sylus to amplify his power. The bleaching is not happening because magic is wrong. It is happening because magic is *trapped* here instead of flowing freely.

Destroying the crystals releases that energy violently. Unpredictable. Potentially catastrophic for Valandir above. Not the answer.

Returning them to the ley line convergence points restores the natural flow. The bleaching retreats — slowly, visibly, permanently.

The Library knows several locations. Most are manageable — Thalira can coordinate distribution over time once proof of concept is established. Two locations are genuinely dangerous and will need the party directly. Those are for later.

**The nearby location:**
There is one convergence point close to Valandir. The Reach — on the city outskirts. Thalira suggests they try it first. Take one crystal fragment. If Mimbi feels the Feywild respond when it is placed, that is all the proof they need. Then the Library takes over the rest.

**The warning Thalira plants:**
*"The crystals only work if they reach the right place intact. A large quantity leaving the city by trade route — scattered, sold, lost — would be a permanent disruption. The ley lines cannot find what they cannot reach. The bleaching would become irreversible."*

She does not know Helios has a warehouse full of crystals ready to ship. The party will connect this themselves.

**Mimbi's decision:** Destroy or return? Hugo's instinct about the mountains has credibility — not the Dwarven mountains specifically, but the principle of returning rather than destroying is correct. Thalira validates this. The ley line thread is the campaign's endgame.

---

### Scene B — The Noble Alliance Crumbles

A messenger arrives while the party wait. Formal clothes, apologetic manner.

One of last night's families is withdrawing. *"Given recent developments, the house feels it prudent to reconsider its position."* Helios's quiet men got there first.

A second message arrives shortly after — different tone entirely. A smaller merchant house. Less to lose, genuine grievance against the BCS.

*"We are still with you. Tell us what you need."*

This family has shipping interests. A sloop in the harbour. They are offering it.

The noble alliance is now one family. But that one family just gave the party their water approach.

---


### Helios Responds

The raven returns. One line.

*"The warehouse. South dock, red lantern above the door. One hour. Bring the prisoner."*

He is drawing them to him. He thinks he controls this. He has Jaunty. He has the home ground. He does not know about the sloop or the sewer.

---


## Part 3 — The Warehouse

### The Basement — The Quiet Part

**One guard** on Jaunty's door. Stealth check DC 13 to take him quietly. Failure means noise — Helios gets suspicious mid-conversation upstairs. Hugo must work harder to cover it.

**Jaunty's room:** Bolted from outside, simple latch. She is conscious and mobile. Frightened but unharmed. The moment she sees the party:

*"There's a crate downstairs. Massive. They're loading it onto a barge at dawn."*

She overheard the guards. That is how the party learn the timeline.

**The crystal crate:** Main basement room. Large, heavy. Two strong characters or a Mage Hand solution needed to move it. Arcana DC 12 — on success the character feels the crystals humming with contained magical energy, warm, clearly not ordinary cargo. Mimbi needs no check. She knows immediately.

**Getting it out:** The harbour access door in the basement opens directly to the water. If the sloop is waiting outside, the crate goes straight onto it. If not, the party need to improvise — the barge Helios hired is also docked here. Commandeering it is audacious but possible.

---

### The Ground Floor — The Meeting

Helios is here. Composed, controlled, but something different underneath. His father's killer is in the party's hands. That is personal.

**His opening offer:**
*"You've been a significant inconvenience. Give me Vaelis and take your housekeeper. Leave Valandir for a year. When you come back this city will have forgotten tonight."*

Almost reasonable. The problem: the crystals stay. He ships them at dawn regardless.

**Rook** stands two steps behind Helios. Two additional guards on the ground floor. Two archers on the gantry above with sightlines across the entire space.

**The signal horn:** Helios has it on his belt. If he uses it, four more men arrive from outside within three rounds. Hugo needs to keep him from reaching it.

**When the fight starts:**
- Rook immediately shields Helios
- Gantry archers open fire on anyone crossing open ground
- Crate corridors provide cover — both sides use them
- The east staircase to the gantry is marked on Vaelis's sketch — clearing the archers is a priority

---

### The Gantry

Two archers. AC 13, HP 28 each. Longbow +4, 1d8+2 piercing. Full sightlines across the ground floor.

Anyone crossing open ground without cover takes fire. The east staircase is the only access — one person wide, committed approach.

Clearing the gantry opens up the ground floor significantly.

---

### Helios's Escape

When the fight turns against him — below half the guards down, or the basement team is heard — Helios moves for the **north wall**.

A false panel. River access. A small private dock with a rowing boat. He planned this before the party arrived.

Helios escapes unless the party specifically cuts off the north wall or someone is already at the river side. This is a decision they have to make in the moment with incomplete information.

*He is not finished. He will be back.*

---

## Helios — Stat Block

*A crime boss, not a soldier. Dangerous in a different way.*

**Helios** — AC 18 (fine clothes, concealed leather with magical enchantment) | HP 78 | Speed 30ft

| Attack | Bonus | Damage | Notes |
|--------|-------|--------|-------|
| Rapier | +6 | 1d8+4 piercing | — |
| Hand crossbow | +6 | 1d6+4 piercing | Range 30/120ft |
| Signal horn | — | — | Bonus action. Calls 4 guards in 3 rounds |

**Commanding Presence:** Once per turn Helios can issue a command to one ally within 30ft. That ally can immediately use their reaction to make one attack.

**Cold Calculation:** Helios has advantage on saving throws against being frightened or charmed.

**Tactics:** Helios does not fight fair and does not fight long. He uses Rook and the guards as a screen, stays at range with the hand crossbow, and moves toward the north wall escape the moment the tide turns. He is not too proud to run. He will be back.

## Part 4 — The Aftermath

**If Jaunty is freed:** She is shaken but intact. She wants to go home. There is no home to go to. That lands.

**If the crystals are secured:** The sloop or barge takes them. Thalira coordinates from the Library. The Reach becomes the next session — Mimbi's proof of concept moment. From there the Library handles distribution and the two dangerous convergence points become the campaign endgame.
