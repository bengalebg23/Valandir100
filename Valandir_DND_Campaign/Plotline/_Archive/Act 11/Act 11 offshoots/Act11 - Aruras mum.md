[[Thalira Barkwood]]

## What Thalira Actually Does
She is part of a quiet internal division inside the Grand Library (or older than it).
Their purpose:
Locate, catalogue, and prevent the activation of Pre-Empire Relic Keys.
Not Fey. Not wild magic. Not surges.
Ancient vault infrastructure.
Before the current kingdoms, there was a civilisation that built:
Sealed treasure repositories
- Power reserves
- Weapon vaults
- Knowledge archives
But they never left maps.
They left Keys.
The jungle totem is one of them.
## What the Totem Really Is
It isn’t treasure.
It’s a Vault Key Anchor.
Think:
Dense carved stone core
Inlaid metallic veins
Rotating ring bands carved with unknown script
A hollow socket at its base
It is incomplete on its own.
When placed into the correct stone housing elsewhere in the world…
It unlocks something vast.
And dangerous.
## Why the Chameleon Guards It
The chameleon isn’t random.
It has been altered by prolonged exposure to the totem.
Heightened camouflage
Venom evolved into neurotoxin
Territorial aggression
Hyper awareness of magical presence
It isn’t “evil.”
It is guarding its territory — and the artifact within it.
The totem subtly warps the ecosystem around it.
Thalira would recognise the signs immediately.
Why the Entrepreneur Wants It
He thinks it leads to treasure.
He’s partially correct.
He does not understand the scale.
He believes:
“First to claim it unlocks a fortune.”
He does not know it is one piece of a global lock system.
## Why This Matters to Thalira
She has been tracking reports of:
Missing relic fragments
Excavated ruins
Black market artifact sales
Now she hears:
An eccentric noble is teleporting teams into a jungle to retrieve a stone relic with rotating rings.
Her blood runs cold.
Because she knows:
Someone is assembling Keys.

When the party returns with the totem (or even just information), Thalira realises:
This isn’t isolated.
The entrepreneur may have been manipulated.
Other teams may not be random adventurers.
This turns:
Fun jungle competition
Into
Quiet arms race for ancient vaults.
