[[Act 8b - into the city and taken in]]

My dear Thicketwhistle,
Your letter brought a great deal of joy to our home. You should have seen your mother’s face when she read that you were so close to the Library — she cried first, then laughed, then made everyone read it twice.
You always did walk toward places of learning without quite meaning to. Your grandmother says that’s because knowledge grows best when it isn’t chased. I think she’s right.
If you truly are near the Archive, then remember this: the old halls were built to keep things, not to judge them. When the world becomes loud, those walls are meant to listen.
We are proud of you — not for where you stand, but for why.
Stay safe. Stay kind. And if doors seem closed, look for the ones that were built before anyone thought to lock them.
With all our love,
Your family
