## Crowe reframes the situation as safety vs chaos, not good vs evil.
1. He invokes Emergency Oversight
Public announcement:
“The AG is assuming temporary control of magical safety.”
This is normal, boring, and legal.
Effect:
Crowe looks responsible, not aggressive.

2. He produces “evidence”
Not proof — correlation.
Lists rift incidents
Notes that:
Surges occurred shortly after the party’s movements
Sylus was nearby in all cases
Effect:
Public doubt, not outrage.

3. He invites the party to cooperate publicly
Key move.
“If you believe you are innocent, submit to AG review.”
This:
Makes refusal look suspicious
Makes Crowe look reasonable

4. He restores minor wards publicly
Lanterns brighter
Small rifts sealed quickly
Effect:
People feel safer after AG steps in.

5. He isolates the party socially
He doesn’t hunt them
He waits
People begin saying:
“Why won’t they just clear this up?”
The party must either:
Submit
Disappear quietly
Find proof fast
