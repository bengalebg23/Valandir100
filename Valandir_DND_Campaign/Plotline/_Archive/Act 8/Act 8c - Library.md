# I. THE AGE OF WARDS (FOUNDATION)
Long ago, Valandir learned a hard lesson:
Magic here does not sit still
It builds, presses, and breaks
When left unchecked, it tears holes between worlds
So a Ward Network was created:
Magic symbols embedded across the land
Each one directing excess ambient magic to a series of resonance crystals
Preventing local surges and planar breaches

# II. THE DWARVEN SOLUTION (STABILITY)
The dwarves provided the answer.
Deep in the mountains grew resonant crystals:
Naturally stabilised
Able to hold planar energy safely
Neither alive nor inert — perfect vessels
With these:
Magic could be absorbed
Stabilised
Stored beneath Valandir in a central lattice
This system worked — for centuries.
# III. THE FIRST FAILURE (THE MINES)
Then two things happened. Quietly.
#### **First: The Veins Thinned**
Crystals became harder to find
New growth slowed
Output dropped year after year
#### **Second: The Plague**
A wasting sickness struck the dwarves
Magic made it worse
Healing spells accelerated death
Crystal resonance caused agony
The dwarves sealed their halls.
Mining slowed. Then stopped.

They did not announce this. They were focused on life. 
# IV. THE LIE OF CONTINUITY
Valandir did not stop growing.
More wards
More enchantments
More dependence on containment

The committee was told what they wanted to hear
“Supply is strained, but sufficient.”
That was untrue.

By the time the truth surfaced:
The crystal lattice was already overloaded

Doing nothing would cause massive failure and no one could predict the consequences 

Reducing the wards started cause catastrophic surges and the AG grew as a consequence 


# V. THE COMMITTEE DEADLOCK
Three proposals were debated for months.
No consensus. No action.
The system continued failing.

"We argued for months.
Not because we lacked answers —
but because every answer we have kills something.”

Then he lists the three options
The three options
1. Human host in the conduit
2. Remove the wards and return to natural
3. Release the wards slowly (pressure valve)

# VI. THE ADVENTURE GUILD’S RESPONSE
The AG was tasked with operational stability, not policy.
Their orders were simple:
“Prevent panic. Prevent collapse.”
Captain Crowe acted.
Outer monoliths were disabled
Magic was allowed to surge in villages
Pressure on the lattice eased temporarily
Rifts followed.
Crowe framed Sylus — not to kill him, but to:
Discredit dissent
Remove him from public view
Deliver him to the Committee alongside Dr Korliss
Sylus was to be contained, not executed.
