[[Act 14 - The Gala]]

The spread is extraordinary. Glazed meats. Pastries with things inside other pastries. Small elegant towers of food that were clearly built to be looked at rather than eaten.
Bwark is eating them anyway.
He has a plate in each hand and is working through the table with the systematic focus of a man who has been underfed for several sessions and intends to correct that tonight. A servant approaches to offer a fresh tray. Bwark takes the entire tray.
A finely dressed woman nearby watches this with an expression cycling slowly through surprise, horror, and reluctant admiration.
And then — across the table — she sees him.
A woman in charcoal grey. Still. Watching Bwark with quiet, deliberate attention while everyone else in the room has turned toward Mimbi's song. She has the look of someone who works for someone else. She catches Bwark's eye and holds it for just a moment.
She places a small folded note on the edge of the table and walks away without a word.
Bwark now has a decision: eat, or read the note.

---
The healer who treated Marcell Veyron on the night of his death is known to us. We have no interest in causing trouble at this event. We have every interest in a quiet conversation before you leave tonight. Come to the side entrance off the east corridor before the speeches end. Come alone. — A friend of Serena Silmore"
----
