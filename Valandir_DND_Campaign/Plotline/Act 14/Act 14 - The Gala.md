### Set the scene 
The Gala
The Grand Hall of the Valandir Library

The hall has been transformed. Lanterns hang in long amber chains from the vaulted ceiling, and the smell of beeswax and expensive wine has replaced the usual dust of old books. Tables groan with food the party has never been able to afford. Servants move like ghosts between clusters of dignitaries — guild officials, library scholars, merchant families, a smattering of minor nobility.

The party are announced at the door by a young steward who reads their names from a card with barely concealed nerves, like he wasn't sure they'd actually show up. A ripple moves through the nearest guests. Some raise glasses. A few deliberately don't.

Jacob is already here, wearing a new coat two sizes too big. He waves furiously from across the room. Kermin Traylow stands near the centre of the hall — heavyset, silver-scaled, dressed in deep blue Library robes. He spots the party immediately and begins making his way over, smoothly intercepting two people who look like they were about to intercept first.

He reaches you before they do, and smiles broadly.

*"You came. Good. I wasn't entirely sure you would."* He glances briefly at the room. *"There are people here who are grateful for what you did. And people here who find gratitude... complicated. Stay close to me for the first half hour and let me do the navigating."*

He hands each of you a glass of wine — real wine, not the tavern kind — and steers the group gently away from the door.

*"Hector de Léon arrived an hour ago,"* he says, quietly and without drama. *"He's been asking about Hugo."*
