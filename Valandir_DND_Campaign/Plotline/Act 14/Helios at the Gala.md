Helios doesn't come to the gala. He's smarter than that — a mob boss crashing a civic celebration is bad optics. Instead, he sends a message *through* the gala. One of the dignitaries — a city merchant Helios owns — quietly pulls Lady Hollingsworth aside during the drinks reception. 

Within minutes, the mood around the party shifts. Hollingsworth looks at them differently. The message is clear: Helios has told the right people that the party lied to him, presented a stranger's head as Garrus, and are now marked. He isn't threatening violence at the gala — he's just closed every door in the city behind them.

The only door left open? Cornelius Thatch. Leave the city on a legitimate expedition, return with something valuable, and come back with enough gold and glory that Helios either forgives the debt or can't touch them publicly. Cornelius — who has been watching all of this unfold with quiet amusement — makes his approach at exactly the right moment.
