[[Act 14 - The Gala]]

# Intro 
He turns to face them fully as they approach, and smiles — genuinely warm, faintly amused, as though the ink-covered druid and the man carrying a catering tray are exactly what he was hoping for.
"Ah. There you are."
He looks at ThicketWhistle for just a moment.
"I see you've met the Mortwood Blackcap."

*"My name is Cornelius Thatch. I am a merchant, an investor, and an enthusiast of things that have been lost for a very long time. I am also, the sponsor of an expedition that departs Valandir in two days."*

He doesn't wait for an explanation. He gestures smoothly toward a quieter alcove off the main hall — a small side room, a door slightly ajar, multiple chairs and a table already set with a decanter and glasses. Like he arranged it an hour ago.

"I wonder if I might have ten minutes of your time. I have a proposition that I think will interest you." A beat, the faint glint of mischief. "And given the evening's trajectory — I suspect you may find yourselves in need of somewhere to be for a while."

He already knows about Helios. That much is clear from the way he says it.


# The note Bwark is holding
Bwark still has Serena's note in his hand when they sit down. Someone — Mimbi, probably — asks what it is. Bwark reads it aloud. 

> "**The healer who treated Marcell Veyron on the night of his death is known to us. We have no interest in causing trouble at this event. We have every interest in a quiet conversation before you leave tonight. Come to the side entrance off the east corridor before the speeches end. Come alone. — A friend of Serena Silmore"**

The room goes quiet. And then Cornelius, who has been listening, says calmly:

"Serena Silmore works with Helios. Has done for years." A pause. "If she knows about your healer, Helios knows. 

And if Helios knows about that... On top of what I've been hearing about a dead man who was seen alive. But whose head was supposedly thrown in the docks?!?

Everything lands at once. The Garrus lie. Bwark's exposure. All of it, in one quiet sentence.

Perfect. Let's run it.

# In the alcove
### The problem (Helios)
The alcove door closes behind them. The noise of the gala drops to a murmur. Cornelius pours six glasses of wine with the unhurried ease of a man in his own home, and settles into the chair opposite the party.

He looks at them each in turn. Lands on ThicketWhistle briefly.

*"I have a towel if you need one."*

Cornelius doesn't react immediately. He listens to the whole thing, then is silent for a moment, turning his wine glass slowly by the stem.

*"Serena Silmore."* He says the name like he's placing a piece on a board. *"She's been in Helios's pocket for the better part of two years. Runs the casino, passes information, takes a cut."* 
A pause.

*"If she knows about your healer — and she clearly does — then Helios knows."*

He looks at Bwark directly, not unkindly.

*"And if Helios knows about that..."*

He sets down his glass and leans forward just slightly.

*"So to summarise your current situation, as I understand it."* He counts on his fingers, almost gently. *"You have deceived one of the most dangerous men in this city. His healer — your colleague — is exposed. And Helios has, within the last hour, sent word to Lady Hollingsworth that your party's continued presence in Valandir is something he considers... a personal inconvenience."*

He picks up his wine again.

*"The doors, as it were, are closing."*

A beat. Then the faint glint returns to his eyes and he sits back, entirely relaxed.

### The Pitch
*"Which brings me rather neatly to why I asked you here."*

He reaches into his coat and produces a small rolled document, tied with dark cord, and places it on the table between them. He doesn't open it yet.

He taps the document once.

*"There is a jungle, two weeks by sea from here, on an island that does not appear on most maps. Within that jungle there is a ruin. Within that ruin there is an artifact — a totem you may call it, it is ancient and extraordinarily valuable —now I intend to retrieve it before anyone else does."*

He glances at the door briefly, then back.

*"I have assembled other teams. Competent people. But hear tale of a particular group with a unique combination of skills, and lucky me they have just risen from the dead!"* 

His eyes move across the group. 

*"Unconventional problem solving. Comfort with improvisation. A demonstrated ability to operate under pressure."* *"And — if I'm being honest — a compelling reason to leave this city for approximately six weeks."*

He unrolls the document. It's a contract. Clean, formal, written in a careful hand. At the bottom, a number.

He turns it to face them.

*"Ten percent of everything recovered from the vault. Passage provided. Supplies provided. Whatever happens with Helios — six weeks is a long time. Cities have short memories when the right people return with the right results."*

He folds his hands again and waits. Entirely patient. Like a man who already knows the answer.

*"So. Shall we discuss the details?"*

---

The contract sits on the table. The muffled sound of Kermin beginning his speech bleeds through the door — they can hear the hall going quiet on the other side.

They have about ten minutes before they're expected back out there.

And somewhere in the main hall, if anyone has reason to glance through the crack in the door — there is a man near the back wall in a charcoal grey coat who has not moved in forty minutes, and who is watching the alcove door with quiet, patient interest.
