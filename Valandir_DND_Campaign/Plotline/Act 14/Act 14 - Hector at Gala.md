Here are specific examples tailored to Hugo’s arc and personality:

### ⚔️ 1. Speaking Instead of Dominating
Action: Hugo chose diplomacy in the cavern instead of finishing the fight, convincing armed enemies to stand down rather than proving superiority.
Father’s View:
“A de Léon does not negotiate from a position of strength — he compels.”
His father would see mercy as weakness and interpret Hugo’s de-escalation as a failure to demonstrate dominance. To him, the point isn’t survival — it’s control.
### 🛡️ 2. Fighting Alongside Commoners as Equals
Action: Hugo treats the party — regardless of birth — as peers and companions rather than subordinates.
Father’s View:
“You were not born to stand shoulder-to-shoulder. You were born to be followed.”
House de Léon likely believes hierarchy is natural and sacred. Hugo valuing loyalty and friendship over rank would deeply unsettle his father.
### ✨ 3. Doubting the Divine “Sign” of His Birth
Action: Hugo has expressed uncertainty about whether the golden surge truly marked him as chosen.
Father’s View:
“The heavens spoke. Only a fool questions what the heavens declare.”
To his father, questioning destiny is not humility — it’s heresy against the family narrative.
### 🩸 4. Being Knocked Unconscious
Action: Hugo has fallen in battle.
Father’s View:
“A symbol cannot fall.”
It wouldn’t matter that others fell too. Hugo was meant to be exceptional. Being seen vulnerable breaks the myth his father carefully cultivated.
### 🤝 5. Showing Emotional Transparency
Action: Hugo shows fear, compassion, doubt, or visible affection toward companions.
Father’s View:
“A leader may feel. He must never display it.”
Emotion is seen as leverage others can exploit. Hugo being emotionally open would be viewed as dangerously naive.
### 🕯️ 6. Staying With the Party Instead of Returning Home
Action: Hugo continues adventuring rather than consolidating influence at home.
Father’s View:
“Glory earned far from witnesses is wasted.”
If Hugo is not building political capital, forging alliances, or expanding House de Léon’s prestige, then he is “playing at heroics.”
### 🗡️ 7. Choosing Who to Protect
Action: Hugo prioritises protecting the weak or innocent rather than strategically important figures.
Father’s View:
“You do not spend gold on gravel.”
His father would measure lives in terms of influence, legacy, and return.
### 👑 8. Defining Himself Outside the House
Action: Hugo builds an identity rooted in personal honour rather than inherited expectation.

Father’s View:
“You are de Léon. There is no ‘outside’ of that.”
Individualism would feel like betrayal.
