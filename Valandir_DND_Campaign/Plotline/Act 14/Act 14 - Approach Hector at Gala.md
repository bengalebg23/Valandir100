[[Act 14 - The Gala]]

Hector isn't hard to find. He's stationed himself near the tall windows at the far end of the hall, a glass of wine held loosely in one hand, speaking with two older men in guild colours. He doesn't look like a man attending a celebration. He looks like a man conducting a meeting in an inconvenient location.
He sees Hugo coming from twenty feet away. He doesn't move toward him. He finishes his sentence to the guild men, and then — only then — turns to face his son.
The two men quietly excuse themselves.
Hector looks Hugo over. Not warmly. The way you assess something you've invested in and are calculating the return on.

"Hugo."

A pause. He lets the noise of the gala fill the silence between them.

"I'm told you broke out of the Adventurers' Guild. That you incited a crowd. That you have been operating in the docklands." 

He takes a measured sip of wine. 

"And that the Library has seen fit to throw a party in your honour."

His expression doesn't change.

"Tell me something. When you imagined what the de Léon name would mean in this city — is this what you pictured?"

---Wait for response---

"You stabilised a city that should never have become unstable. You exposed an institution that should never have required exposing. You fought battles that should never have needed fighting." 


A beat.

"And at each step, you did it loudly, publicly, and at considerable personal cost — when a de Léon with half your ability and twice your patience could have solved the same problems from a seat at the right table."
He turns slightly, looking out at the room. His voice drops just a fraction.
"That man over there — Aldric Voss, guild treasurer — I had breakfast with him this morning. In one conversation I secured the Library's eastern funding contract and quietly buried an inquiry that would have complicated your situation considerably." He looks back at Hugo. "No crowd. No carnivale. No broken market stalls."
He tilts his head almost imperceptibly.
"You have courage, Hugo. I have never questioned that. But courage without architecture is just noise."
He takes one more sip of wine.
"Your mother asks after you."
And just like that he's moving on — not storming off, not dismissing Hugo with a gesture. Simply done, the way you close a ledger. He takes two steps before pausing without turning back.
