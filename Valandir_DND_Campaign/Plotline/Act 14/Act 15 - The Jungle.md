Jungle Quest Crib Sheet (3 Sessions)
# Session 1 – Preparation & Departure
Goal: Hook the party, set the stakes, and allow them to gather essential tools and knowledge.
Cornelius’ Role: Polished, refined; presents the jungle as a race against other teams. Offers suggestions for preparation rather than dictating exactly what to bring.
Suggested Player Prompts / How to Suggest Artifacts
Cornelius gestures to a table of items in his study or at the gala: “Some tools may prove… invaluable. Rare maps, enchanted lanterns, protective charms. Choose wisely; the jungle rewards the clever.”

Example phrasing:
“A compass that always points to true north can mean the difference between getting lost and finding the path.”
“Some herbs repel creatures. Others heal. Even the most mundane-looking potions may save your life.”
“Ancient keys and charms resonate with ruins… sometimes they react when you most need them.”

## Suggested Artifacts / Supplies
- Verdant Sigil Compass – Points subtly toward the location of druidic ruins.
- Herbal Antivenom / Jungle Remedies – Cures poisons or disease common in the jungle.
- Weatherproof Map or Magical Map – Reveals changes in the jungle terrain as the party moves.
- Protective Amulet / Charm – Reduces environmental or magical hazards.
- Light Source / Luminous Gem – Illuminates ruins, attracts or repels certain creatures.


### Optional Prep Encounters:
- Rival scouts in town: the party may have minor social encounters with competing teams to hint at rivalry.
- A puzzle or research challenge: decipher hints about jungle terrain, flora, or minor magical wards.

# Session 2 – Jungle Travel & Encounters
Goal: Explore jungle, introduce environmental hazards, minor treasure, and rival teams.
Arrival
Jungle is dense, humid, alive with wildlife sounds, shafts of light breaking through the canopy.
Describe: sounds of distant roars, hanging vines, massive trees, occasional glimpses of ruins.
Travel Mechanics / Tips
Travel is slow; require occasional navigation checks (e.g., Survival or Nature) to avoid getting lost.
Random environmental effects: sudden storms, mudslides, poisonous plants, or swarms of insects.
Day/night cycle: certain creatures or ruins are only active at night.

### Encounters
Wildlife / Predators: Giant snakes, venomous spiders, territorial cats, or magical jungle beasts.
Environmental Hazards: Quickmud, falling trees, river crossings, poisonous flora.
Ruins & Puzzles: Minor druidic shrines with simple riddles or warded doors. Can reward minor artifacts or clues to the Verdant Sigil’s location.

### Rival Parties:
Can appear at key moments; may try to sabotage, race ahead, or engage diplomatically.
You can roleplay them as mirrors of the party, highlighting competition without always fighting.

# Session 3 – Finding the Key & Climactic Challenges
Goal: Reach the Verdant Sigil, overcome the jungle’s final defenses, and deal with rival teams.

## The Key / Artifact
Location: Ancient ruins, hidden in a chamber protected by nature’s magic.
Challenges:
Traps / Wards: Vine snare, magical glyphs, guardian spirits.
Tests of Skill / Wit: Environmental puzzles, creature negotiation, skill challenges.

Verdant Sigil reacts to items the party brought: charms, compass, or magical map can trigger clues or bypass hazards.

### Final Encounters
Competing Teams: Could reach the ruins simultaneously; tension, negotiation, or conflict may arise.
Guardian Creature / Nature Challenge: A powerful jungle elemental, magical beast, or ancient sentinel tied to the Sigil.
Environmental Hazard: Flooding chamber, collapsing ruins, poison gas, or shifting terrain.

### Resolution / Rewards
Party obtains Verdant Sigil (or fails, forcing retreat or negotiation).
Rival teams: some may escape, some may be thwarted, creating long-term consequences for the city or future quests.
Loot / XP / Glory: reward artifacts, treasures, or knowledge discovered during exploration


# 🦎 The Chameleon
This is not just a beast.
It is a territorial apex ambush predator.
It does not fight head-on.
It hunts by:
Patience
Poison
Misdirection
Core Concept
As teams approach the ruin, the jungle subtly changes.
Not obvious at first.
Colours slightly oversaturated.
Air humid and heavy.
Small insects unnaturally quiet.
The Chameleon releases a slow aerosolised venom through throat sacs.
Not magical. Biological.
It disperses into the canopy.
The closer you get to the totem’s clearing, the denser it becomes.

## ☠ The Poison Mechanic
Not instant damage.
This is creeping impairment.
### Phase 1 – Exposure
CON save every 10–15 minutes in affected zone.
On first failure:
Disadvantage on Perception checks (colours blur)
Mild euphoria or paranoia
### Phase 2 – Neurological Drift
Second failure:
Movement reduced
Visual distortions
Can’t tell distance well
### Phase 3 – Paralytic Tremor
Third failure:
Poisoned condition
Risk of restrained/paralysed if untreated
Cure?
Neutralise Poison works.
Herbal antidote if they harvest jungle flora (Nature check).
Moving upwind helps.
Covering mouths helps.
Reward intelligence, not brute force.

### 🏕 The Other Team
When the party finds them:
One rival team is already affected.
Symptoms:
One unconscious.
One hallucinating.
One insisting something is moving in the trees.
You can:
Let players rescue them.
Or learn from their failure.
Or interrogate them for what they saw.
This shows the threat without killing momentum.

### 🦎 Camouflage
The Chameleon should be nearly impossible to see at first.
Mechanics:
Advantage on Stealth.
Only visible when:
It moves.
It attacks.
Someone succeeds a very high Perception check.
Or clever use of flour, dust, paint, firelight, etc.
Encourage:
Smoke to reveal air distortion.
Splashed mud.
Cutting vines to reduce cover.
Setting controlled fire lines.
Using sound bait.
The fight should feel like:
They are hunting the hunter.
### 🗿 The Totem
Now the important part.
You want it to be a key to an almighty treasure.
Not Fey. Not planar. Not surge-related.
Good.
Let’s make it ancient jungle civilisation.
The Totem Concept
The totem is not the treasure.
It is:

### A calibration key.
When placed in the correct socket in a distant ruin, it unlocks:
The Vault of Khar’zeth (name flexible)
An underground pre-imperial treasury.
Sealed for centuries.
The jungle grew over it.
The Chameleon is the final evolutionary guardian.
Not created. Not magical. Just territorial over its domain.
The totem radiates faint mineral resonance.
The Chameleon evolved to guard it because:
It nests in the warm stone.
It feeds on animals drawn to the clearing.
The clearing provides heat and vantage.
No cosmic destiny.
Just ecology.

### 🏆 The Prize
The entrepreneur’s prize could be:
Ownership claim to the expedition rights.
A share in the vault’s contents.
Exclusive exploration contract.
10,000+ gold equivalent.
Political favour with trade guilds.
But he hasn’t told them everything.
He believes the vault contains:
Lost gold hoards.
Relic weaponry.
Or something even rarer…
Ancient trade maps to a forgotten continent.

## 🧠 How the Encounter Plays
Stage 1: Poison clues. Rival team failure.
Stage 2: Discovery of poison dispersal pattern. Environmental counterplay.
Stage 3: Ambush from canopy. Hit-and-run attacks. Tongue grapple / tail sweep.
Stage 4: Reveal. It drops camouflage under stress. Final intelligent fight.
No brawl in open field.
Vertical battlefield.

## 🔥 Make It Tactical
Give the clearing:
40 ft tall trees
Hanging vines
Ruined stone pillars
Dense undergrowth (difficult terrain)
Pollen clouds (visibility issues)
It should reward:
Ranged combat.
Control spells.
Movement creativity.
