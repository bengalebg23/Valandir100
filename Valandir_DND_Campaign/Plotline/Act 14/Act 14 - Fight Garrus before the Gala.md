[[Act 14 - The Gala]]

This is a comedy brawl, not a lethal encounter. Garrus and his friends are drunk, angry, and wearing their adventuring gear but in no state to use it properly.

Mechanically I'd suggest:
All four have disadvantage on attack rolls — they keep second-guessing themselves or stumbling mid-swing

Garrus has one moment of accidental competence — one solid hit on whoever's nearest, purely by luck, which he is disproportionately proud of

One of the friends sits down halfway through and refuses to continue on the grounds that this isn't what he signed up for tonight
The tankard friend never puts the tankard down and fights entirely one-handed

If anyone tries to reason with Garrus mid-combat he briefly considers it, then remembers he was nearly murdered and gets angry again

The punchline ending — just as it's winding down, the carriages finally arrive. Two smart horses, a liveried driver, sent by Kermin. The driver looks at the scene — the party in formal wear, Garrus on the floor, one man still holding a tankard — and says nothing. He's clearly been driving for the Library long enough to have seen things.

He simply opens the carriage door.

## The Consequence
Helios's watchers saw the whole thing. They saw Garrus. They heard his name shouted repeatedly at volume. By the time the party arrive at the gala, Helios already has a second report confirming his man is very much alive and apparently furious about it.
It also means the party arrive at the gala slightly dishevelled, possibly with a bruise or two, and five minutes late. Kermin spots them from across the room, clocks the state of them, and closes his eyes briefly in the manner of a man who is very tired.
