The canapés are excellent. ThicketWhistle knows this because he has eaten four of them off a passing tray without the servant noticing, largely because he is now approximately the height of the tray and moving with it.
He has also, in the last ten minutes:

— released his crow familiar into the rafters, where it is eyeing the chandeliers with what can only be described as intent

— discovered that one of the Library's display cases contains a very small and very old mushroom specimen, and is now working out whether the latch is purely decorative

— made eye contact with a guild official's wife, held it for an uncomfortable four seconds, and then vanished behind a pillar

The crow drops a single feather onto the wig of a senior alderman. ThicketWhistle watches from behind the pillar, delighted.



The latch is not purely decorative. ThicketWhistle establishes this when he touches it and a faint blue ring pulses outward from the case like a ripple in still water. Nobody nearby notices. ThicketWhistle notices.

He examines it. It's a containment ward — old Library magic, the kind that's been sitting dormant so long it's almost polite. A DC 14 Arcana check would tell him it's a stasis seal, designed to preserve the specimen inside.
It would also tell him, on a 16 or higher, that there's a secondary enchantment underneath. A deterrent.

ThicketWhistle does not make this check. ThicketWhistle opens the latch.

The ink bomb is small, efficiently designed, and has been waiting patiently for approximately sixty years.

It does not explode dramatically. It does not make a sound. It simply releases — a dense black cloud that exists entirely within a two foot radius of the case and deposits itself, with almost surgical precision, entirely onto ThicketWhistle.

He is now standing in the middle of the Valandir memorial gala looking like he lost a fight with a printing press. Face. Hands. The good coat. All of it.

The crow, from the rafters, makes a sound that is almost certainly laughing.
A nearby scholar peers down at him over her glasses.

"...Did you touch the Mortwood Blackcap?"
