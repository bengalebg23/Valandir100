# Session 16 — Scene: The Reach

*(Follows the Main Square scene. Party has the crystal from Kermin/Thalira. Short rest only. No long rest.)*

---

## Arriving at the Portal

The party arrives at the threshold of Barkfield Reach — familiar ground. They've been here before. They know the protocol.

Thrynn is waiting outside the portal as expected, flanked by two or three druids. She greets them without ceremony — she's not warm, but she's not hostile either. This is routine.

> *"You have the crystal. Good. Leave your arms and armour here."*

The party disarms. They've done this before. There's no reason to be worried.

Thrynn opens the portal and leads them through into the Reach.

---

## Inside the Reach

The convergence point is deeper in — Thrynn leads them through the familiar landscape of the Reach, her druids falling into a loose perimeter. She explains where the crystal needs to be placed and what the ritual requires. It's not complex — Mimbi will know what to do.

She leaves them to it and takes a position at the edge of the clearing.

---

## The Ritual Begins

Mimbi begins placing the crystal at the convergence point. The land responds immediately — a low hum, barely audible, the grass bending slightly toward the point as the ley line recognises what's happening.

**One round passes.** Nothing unusual. The hum deepens.

**Then Thrynn goes still.**

She turns her head toward the portal. Something is wrong.

> *"Someone is at the boundary."*

She moves — fast for someone her age — back toward the portal threshold, two druids following. She tells the party over her shoulder:

> *"Keep going. Do not stop the placement."*

---

## The Portal Threshold — What Thrynn Finds

Soren Mourne is standing at the boundary of the ward. Calm. He is holding a crystal — not the party's crystal. His own, harvested from the Velmoran operation underground. It glows faintly in his grip.

Thrynn plants herself in his path.

> *"You have no right of passage here."*

Soren looks at her the way a surgeon looks at a door he needs to open.

He casts **Suggestion**, amplified through the crystal. DC 17 Wisdom save. Thrynn fails.

> *"Step aside and let me through. This won't take long."*

She steps aside. Her own ward does not register a threat — its guardian invited him in. He walks through without touching her.

The crystal dims as he releases it. He pockets it and continues into the Reach.

---

## The Eruption

Back at the convergence point, the ritual is mid-flow when the ground splits.

**A1 and A2 erupt** from the earth — the ley line energy destabilising the substrate, driving ancient creatures upward. The party is unarmed.

Thrynn, Suggestion wearing off as Soren clears the threshold, realises what she's done. She sprints back toward the party — but she has to cross the Reach to reach them. She's not going to make it in time.

**Thrynn sends her druids ahead of her. They return the party's weapons and armour immediately — the sanctuary rule is suspended. This is an emergency.**

---

## The Fight

**Round 1–2:** Party fights A1 and A2 with returned gear. The Ankhegs grapple — bite auto-hits grappled targets. A3–A6 can be felt below the surface, tremorsense, movement in the earth.

**A3–A6 begin to surface.**

Then Soren steps into the clearing.

He assesses the situation in one glance — the Ankhegs, the party mid-fight, the crystal still pulsing at the convergence point, Mimbi affected by the ley line surge.

He raises the gauntlet. No preamble. No question to the party.

**Fires.** Two charges. A3–A6 are blasted mid-emergence. They do not survive.

---

## After the Fight

Silence except for the hum of the crystal settling into the convergence point.

Mimbi takes the surge — **1d6 force damage** passes through her as the crystal locks in. She feels it more than it hurts.

Soren watches this with undisguised interest. He takes out a small journal. Makes a note.

> *"The conduit effect. Fascinating. I hadn't expected a living resonant at this stage."*

He is not speaking to the party. He is speaking to himself.

---

## Thrynn Arrives

Thrynn enters the clearing. She takes in the scene — the Ankhegs dead, Soren standing among them, the crystal placed. She is breathing hard. The two druids at her back are visibly shaken.

She looks at Soren. He meets her gaze.

She does not speak to him.

She turns to the party.

> *"He used the land's own power against me. On my ground."*

That is all she says about it.

---

## Soren's Offer

He turns to the party now. The journal goes away.

> *"I thought you might need assistance."*

He makes his case — measured, reasonable, infuriating:

- The ley line energy is being wasted on restoration when it could be *understood*, *harnessed*, used to prevent this kind of rupture from happening again
- The Velmoran Institute has the expertise to manage it responsibly
- The Feywild will stabilise on its own eventually — it always has
- He will compensate them. Generously. No further legal action regarding the dock

He does not mention Thrynn. He does not acknowledge what he did to her.

He waits for their answer.

---

## The Party Refuses

He nods. Expected.

> *"This isn't over."*

He leaves the Reach the way he came. The ward closes behind him as if he was never here.

---

## What This Scene Establishes

- Soren is genuinely, quietly terrifying — he defeated the guardian of a druidic sanctuary without raising his voice
- The crystals amplify his power in ways the party has now witnessed directly
- Thrynn has been humiliated on her own ground and will not forget it — future ally with a personal stake
- Mimbi's conduit effect is documented in Soren's journal — he knows what she is now
- The party leaves the Reach knowing Soren will be at the Watchtower too
