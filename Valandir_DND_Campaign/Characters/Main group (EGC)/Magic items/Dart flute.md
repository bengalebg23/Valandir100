Nice. This is exactly the sort of thing a clever gnome bard would quietly stock up on before trouble starts. Since the dart is tiny, Mimbi would probably favour fast-acting toxins that work in small doses rather than big battlefield poisons.
Here are some good options she’d realistically know to look for:
Serpent Venom
Classic adventurer poison
Cost: ~200 gp
Use: Action to apply (coats 1 dart)
Effect: On hit, target makes DC 11 CON save
Damage: 3d6 poison on a fail, half on success
Why Mimbi buys it:
Reliable, widely known, and works even in very small doses.
Drow Poison
The subtle favourite
Cost: ~200 gp
Use: Action to apply
Effect: Target makes DC 13 CON save
Fail: Poisoned for 1 hour
Fail by 5+: Unconscious for 1 hour
Why Mimbi buys it:
Perfect for a stealth dart from the flute. Very bard-appropriate.
Oil of Taggit
The knockout option
Cost: ~400 gp
Use: Action to apply
Effect: Target makes DC 13 CON save
Fail: Unconscious for 24 hours
Takes damage = wakes up
Why Mimbi buys it:
Great for kidnapping, sneaking past guards, or ending fights quietly.
Midnight Tears
The slow, dramatic one
Cost: ~1500 gp
Effect: If ingested, damage triggers at midnight
9d6 poison damage
Why Mimbi knows it:
Not ideal for darts, but a bard absolutely knows this one for assassination or intrigue.
Feysleep Powder (great for your campaign flavour)
Favoured by Fey tricksters
Cost: ~150 gp
Use: Coat a dart
Effect: Target makes DC 12 CON save
Fail: Falls asleep for 1 minute
Wakes if damaged
Why Mimbi buys it:
Non-lethal and mischievous. Very bard energy.
Suggested Rule for the Flute Darts
Because the darts are so small:
Each vial of poison coats 3 darts
Applying poison during combat = Bonus Action
Outside combat = trivial
