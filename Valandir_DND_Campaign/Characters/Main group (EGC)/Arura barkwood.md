 Arura (Ah-roar-ah) — Backstory
 [[Heartroot of the Ironstone Grove]]

Arura was born beneath the towering stone steps of the Grand Library of Valandir, the very steps her father, [[Cain Barkwood]], had crafted with his own hands. He was a human stone merchant—steady, grounded, hardworking. To Arura, he was a hero made of granite and good intentions, a man whose dream was simple: for his daughter to inherit the family craft and expand the Ironstone name until it echoed through all of Dinah.

Her mother, [[Thalira Barkwood]], was anything but simple. A wandering wood elf scholar, she had come to the capital in search of ancient truths about her people and the source of their magic. She found something else entirely—Cain. Their connection was instant: the curious elf with eyes like dawn, and the humble mason with hands that shaped the world. From that unlikely union came Arura.

Growing up half-elf in Valandir was never easy, but magic made it harder. Arura’s powers burst from her like startled birds—wild, sudden, untrained. Sparks where there should have been silence. Illusions where there should have been concentration. The other children didn’t understand, and so they pushed her away. That loneliness forged a fierce curiosity in her, driving her to explore the woods surrounding the city, where she could practice in peace and hear her thoughts without judgment.

Cain tried to guide her gently toward his trade. He believed her magic would make her the city’s greatest merchant—able to read people, to charm them, to help the business flourish beyond anything he ever achieved. But the truth he never found the words to say was simpler and far more painful: he feared for her. The Society had a reputation—danger, death, strange missions whispered about in taverns late at night. Cain’s tongue stumbled whenever he tried to explain this. Every argument came out as frustration instead of fear. Yet Arura always respected him, even when she didn’t understand him.
studies in the Grand Library became her world. She loved her daughter, unquestionably—but knowledge pulled her deeper than she intended. She always imagined Arura would follow in her footsteps too, becoming a scholar, a keeper of history. The idea of her daughter joining the Society frightened her. It meant risk, conflict, and stepping away from both parents. Still, Thalira never truly confronted Arura about it; she simply receded into her research, hoping her daughter would stay safely between the pages of books and the patterns of stone.

But Arura had her own path.

The Society represented possibility—freedom from expectations, from the gentle cages her parents had unknowingly built. It was a place where magic was not a burden, but a tool. A place where she could make a difference. So she set out to prove herself: not just to the Society, but to her parents… and perhaps, to herself.

She still roams the forests outside Valandir, grounding her emotions in nature and training her abilities where no one can judge her. She longs to show her parents that she is more than what they fear, more than what they want, and far more than they ever imagined.
