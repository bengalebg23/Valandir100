Minbi Drugledamp — Forest Gnome Bard (with Fey Magic)

Minbi Drugledamp is a wandering bard-for-hire, a tiny forest gnome with a presence far larger than her stature. To most, she’s a spellbinding performer whose mastery of instruments can quiet a tavern or rouse a battlefield. But her true strength lies not in her showmanship — it lies in the subtle, ancient threads of fey magic woven into every melody she plays.

Though around 250 years old, Minbi retains the youthful appearance of a much younger gnome. Her skin has a warm olive tone, and her bronze hair is intricately braided with living ivy and dainty pink blossoms that never seem to wilt. A small, pointy nose and large golden eyes give her a look that feels slightly otherworldly — more refined and glamorous than the rugged, earth-bound aesthetic of most forest gnomes.

People stare, she lets them, she thrives on it. All the world's a stage after all.

Minbi has travelled the lands aligning herself with many adventurers and travellers hiring her for her songs and mystery. And yet those who bring her into the fold find themselves getting the best luck of their lives. 

Minbi's advanced age and appearance marks her out to those in the know as touched by fey magic. She can harness fey magic through her ancestral connection to the Feywild. Refined Lore: Mimbi of the Bleached Bough
Mimbi is two hundred and fifty years old—barely halfway through the life she should have lived.
Her fey blood stretches her years, but it also binds her to something older and more fragile than flesh: zest—the spark of joy, curiosity, and emotional intensity that sustains the Fey.
Long ago, when the rift between the Feywild and the mortal world stood wide and breathing, this zest flowed freely. Fey crossed often, laughter had weight, sorrow had colour, and magic was ambient—wild, unmetered, alive.
Then magic was controlled.
Wards were raised. Leys were bound. The world was made efficient.
And the link to the Fey thinned.
Among the elders of Mimbi’s people, a wasting began—called Bleaching.
Not sickness. Not age.
A quiet draining of colour, passion, memory. Fey who forgot why they once danced. Who smiled less. Who aged not in body at first, but in spirit. When the zest is gone, the years arrive all at once.
Believing distance might preserve her, Mimbi left her kin.
She wandered the mortal world seeking new zest—stories, danger, love, mischief—anything to keep the Bleaching at bay.
That search led her to the Grand Library.
There, she discovered the truth too late:
The Library does not merely record magic.
It stabilises it.
It siphons excess emotion, wild inspiration, raw narrative potential—everything the Fey require to remain themselves. Each catalogue completed, each ward reinforced, each spell indexed… bleeds the Fey realms a little drier.
The Library is not malicious.
It is thorough.
And through it, her entire family—elders, mentors, ancestors—were slowly undone.
Mimbi did not abandon her people.
She was sent away so she would survive long enough to learn who was killing them.
