*🐔 Bwark the Unwitting Blade* 

 *The Name* 
Bwark’s name was never chosen — it was bestowed, shouted by street urchins and whispered by tavern drunks who mocked his plague doctor mask’s beaked silhouette. “Looks like a chicken,” they’d cackle. “Bwark bwark!” But Bwark, ever earnest, took it as a compliment. Chickens, after all, are vigilant, communal, and surprisingly fierce when cornered. He believes the name honors his tenacity and healing instincts. He even signs his vials with a little feather glyph.

 *The Coincidence* 
Bwark is a street healer — not a warrior, not a vigilante. He sets bones, brews poultices, and dispenses mercy in the alleys of plague-ridden towns. But something strange began to happen: patients he treated started turning up dead. Not just any patients — bad ones. Murderers. Thieves. Men who had escaped justice for years. The deaths were never traced to him. No poison, no wounds. Sometimes they’d die days later, sometimes in their sleep. But the pattern was undeniable: if Bwark treated you and you had blood on your hands, your days were numbered.

Rumors spread. Some say he’s a divine instrument of judgment. Others whisper of a hidden vigilante who follows his trail. A few believe his vials contain spirits that hunt the wicked. Bwark, meanwhile, just thinks he’s finally getting recognition for his healing work.

 *The Hall of Recruits* 
Bwark now stands in the grand marble hall of the Adventurer’s Society, surrounded by hopeful recruits wielding swords, staves, and spells. He’s here because he misread a flyer that said:

 _“Join the fight against monsters and villains. Free lunch provided.”_ 

Bwark, who hasn’t eaten in two days, arrived promptly at dawn with his staff and a pouch of healing vials, expecting soup and maybe a few sick villagers to tend to. He’s now being evaluated for combat readiness.

Little does he know, his reflexes — honed from dodging thrown chamber pots and wrestling plague-maddened patients — make him surprisingly adept in a fight. His fists, hardened from years of grinding herbs and cracking ribs back into place, carry more force than he realizes.

 *Current Status* 
Bwark is confused but polite. He’s offered to check the blood pressure of the half-orc barbarian next to him. The recruiters are watching him closely. One whispers, “That’s Bwark — the one whose patients keep dying. We need him.”
