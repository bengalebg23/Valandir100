The [[BSC]]
A crime syndicate built on vice, leverage, and quiet control — not open brutality.
Overview
The Gilded Knuckle is one of the most entrenched criminal organisations in Valandir’s capital districts and river trade wards. Unlike obvious thugs or cultish factions like the Ashen Hand, the [[BC]] operates with polish, contracts, and plausible deniability.
They are merchants of pressure.
Where there is debt, addiction, blackmail, or quiet desperation — the [[BC]] is already there.

## Symbol & Calling Card
Their symbol is a gold signet ring shaped like stylised crown, worn by captains and lieutenants.
Victims who “default” sometimes receive a small gold-pressed wax seal stamped with a crown — a reminder that the [[BC]] does not forget.

## Structure
The Knuckle is structured more like a mercantile guild than a gang:
The Ledgerlord
The unseen head of the organisation.
Known only by title.
Rumoured to:
Be a former noble financier
Sit on a legitimate trade council
Or possibly be several people rotating the role
No one below Captain level has ever seen them directly.
The Captains
Each controls a sector:
River Trade & Smuggling
Debt Houses & Gambling
Alchemy & Street Narcotics
“Security Services” (extortion dressed as protection)
Captains compete quietly — never violently — because instability costs coin.
The Collectors
Well-dressed enforcers who specialise in:
Contract intimidation
Social sabotage
Ruining reputations
Seizing property legally (or nearly legally)
They prefer breaking livelihoods to breaking bones.
Methods of Control
The Gilded Knuckle rarely burns buildings or stages massacres. That’s amateur work.
Instead, they:
Buy up failing businesses before bankrupting rivals
Manipulate dock schedules to ruin independent merchants
Control alchemical supply chains
Offer “loans” that quietly enslave entire families
They operate inside the law as often as outside it.
Which makes them harder to fight.
What Bwark Did
And this is where it gets fun.
Bwark didn’t set out to dismantle them.
He simply:
Healed dockworkers who had been injured in suspicious “accidents.”
Helped addicts detox from alchemical dependence.
Exposed a falsified debt ledger (by accident).
Publicly refused to hand over a patient who “owed money.”
Each act was small.
But together?
He disrupted:
Their fear narrative.
Their dependency model.
Their income from desperation.
And worse…
People started whispering that the Knuckle was afraid of him.
Why They Haven’t Killed Him
Killing Bwark would:
Turn him into a martyr.
Unite the poor districts.
Draw Adventurer Guild or noble scrutiny.
Instead, they’ve tried:
Smearing his reputation.
Fabricating legal charges.
Turning former patients against him.
Hiring intermediaries to “test” him.
If he survives long enough, the Ledgerlord may decide he is either:
Too dangerous to ignore.
Or too useful not to recruit.
Campaign Hooks
A Captain wants Bwark dead — quietly.
A Collector offers him a contract: heal their people exclusively.
The Ledgerlord requests a private audience.
The Knuckle begins targeting his allies instead.
A rival syndicate moves in, blaming him for destabilisation.
