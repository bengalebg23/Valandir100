Warden of [[Barkfield Reach]]
Race: Dwarf
Age: Old by dwarf standards (120+), weathered rather than frail
Role: Archdruid / Keeper of the Reach
Symbol: A split-stone talisman overgrown with living bark
Visual: Thick beard braided with leaves and bone-charms, deep scars along her forearms where bark has fused permanently into flesh
“Roots do not forget the stone that crushed them.”

Origin & Philosophy
Thrynn was born beneath the mountains and trained in the ancient Dwarven wardcraft — the original form, meant to guide magic, not bind it.
When the great plagues and surges began:
The AG and Library overcorrected
Wards became cages
Ambient magic was throttled rather than tuned
Thrynn walked away from the holds in protest.
She believes:
Magic must breathe
Suppression breeds rupture
Balance requires constant stewardship, not permanent locks
This makes her:
Critical of the AG
Furious at the Library’s arrogance
Deeply opposed to Sylus’ recklessness
“You do not heal a cracked dam by blowing it apart.”
🔥 Relationship to Sylus
Thrynn trained Sylus when he first came to Barkfall Reach — broken, grieving, angry.
She taught him:
How to read the aura
How to feel the land’s pain
Why dwarves once sang to their wards
But she refused to teach him how to break them.
Sylus left when she told him:
“If you tear the roots free, the forest dies with them.”
She regrets not stopping him sooner — but will not excuse him.
