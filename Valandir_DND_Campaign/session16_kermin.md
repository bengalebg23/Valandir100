# Session 16 — Scene: Kermin's Briefing

*(This scene precedes "Session 16 — Opening Scene: The Main Square". Chronologically: Kermin briefing → Main Square → travel → The Reach.)*

---

## Context

The party has just delivered the crystal crate to Thalira at the Library. Before they can properly process what just happened, they're called — or they go looking — to see Kermin about the next job. It is still early morning. Short rest only, no long rest.

---

## The Scene

Kermin's office, or wherever he conducts guild business, is busier than usual — word is already spreading about Helios. He doesn't dwell on it. There's a job.

> *"Good work at the dock. I'll let the city celebrate that one without me — I've got a job that doesn't wait for parades."*

He lays out what he knows:

> *"The Library needs a crystal taken out to the Reach. One of the old convergence points east of the city. It's not glamorous. Walk it out, place it, walk back. Should be a few hours, there and back, if the ground cooperates."*

He doesn't oversell it. As far as he knows, this is a delivery job — not a combat job. That matters: the party should walk into the Reach without full information about what's waiting underground.

> *"Thalira's people will have the crystal ready. Talk to her on your way out — she'll have the details I don't."*

---

## What Kermin Does NOT Know

He has no idea about the Ankhegs, the rift disturbance, or Soren. As far as the guild's records go, the Reach is an old, quiet site. This is deliberate — it lets the eruption hit the table as a real surprise rather than a foreshadowed beat.

---

## Optional Beat — Acknowledging the Dock

If it fits the table's energy, Kermin can briefly note the diplomatic ending at the dock:

> *"Heard you let most of them walk. That's... not how this guild usually does things. Don't let it become a habit. But I won't pretend it wasn't smart."*

Keep this short. It's a nod, not a debate.

---

## Transition

Once they have the job and the crystal, they leave the guild hall — and walk straight into the Main Square scene already in your notes. Bells ringing, crowds celebrating, Lady Hollingsworth mid-speech, and Soren's interruption.

---

## What This Scene Establishes

- The Reach job is framed as low-stakes and routine — sets up the eruption as a genuine escalation
- No long rest before the Reach — the party is working on a short rest and whatever resources survived the dock fight
- Kermin is pragmatic, slightly impressed despite himself, and not a font of information on the supernatural side of the job
