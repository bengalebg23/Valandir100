# Final Session — The City Under Siege & The Portal

*(Follows: Kermin briefing → Main Square → The Reach. This session begins after a long rest. Soren has escalated — the Watchtower convergence point is bypassed; the campaign closes in a single combined finale at the Portal.)*

---

## Opening — The Ransacked Library

The party returns from the Reach expecting to rest and regroup. Instead, the city tells them something is wrong before they even reach the Library.

Smoke. Too much noise. People running the wrong direction.

The Library has been hit. Soren teleported in a host of Velmoran battle-mages and threw them directly at the Adventurers' Guild and the Library simultaneously — a coordinated strike, not a raid. He was looking for the crystals.

### The Scene

- AG members are down — wounded, not necessarily dead, but the guild hall is a mess of broken furniture and worse
- The Library is ransacked — shelves torn down, Thalira's research scattered, anything that looked like it might be a crystal cache broken open and searched
- Thalira is alive but shaken. She got the crystals out in time — barely.

> *"He came himself. Not just his people — him. He went through every ward we had like they were paper."*

She tells them what happened: the moment word reached him that the Library held the bulk of the remaining crystals, he didn't wait, didn't negotiate, didn't send proxies. He came in person with enough force to make the Library's defences irrelevant.

> *"This isn't a man waiting for an answer any more. This is a man who has decided the answer doesn't matter."*

### The Decision Point

Thalira tells them plainly: there is no more time for the staged, careful approach. The Watchtower convergence point will have to wait — there's no safe way to get crystals there now, not with Soren actively hunting for them in the city.

> *"We finish this today, at the portal, with everything we have left — or we lose what's left to him entirely."*

This is the pivot into the merged finale. The party needs to get the remaining crystals out of the city to the portal site before Soren finds them.

---

## Hiding the Crystals — Thrynn's Warding

Thalira doesn't have an answer for how to move the crystals without Soren's people sensing them — but she knows who might.

**Thrynn Rootscar** reappears here. She has not forgotten what Soren did to her at the Reach, and when she hears what he's done to the city, she comes to the party rather than waiting to be asked.

> *"You cannot hide power from someone hunting for power. You hide it from someone hunting for a *signature*."*

She explains the old dwarven wardcraft — not the suppression magic the Library and AG have leaned on for years, but the original craft, the kind meant to guide and mute rather than cage. She binds the remaining crystals in **dwarven warding cloth** — bark-and-stone work, old methods, woven specifically to flatten a magical resonance into something that reads as inert stone to detection magic.

> *"This will not stop him forever. It will stop him today. Today is what you need."*

This is also closure for her arc — what was used against her at the Reach (her own ward, turned against her) is answered here by something he can't simply talk his way past. The warding isn't a spell he can Suggestion his way through. It's craft, not magic, and it doesn't respond to him at all.

**Mechanically:** while warded, the crystals are undetectable by Soren's usual means (divination, his crystal-amplified senses, anything magical). The party can move them to the portal site without him knowing where they are — until the moment Mimbi begins the ritual and the crystals' own energy starts interacting with the convergence point, which the warding cannot mute.

---

## The Portal — Final Encounter

### Setup

The party reaches the portal site with the warded crystals. The moment the ritual begins and the final crystal touches the convergence point, the warding's purpose is spent — the energy here is no longer something that can be hidden. This is the last placement. It was always going to be loud.

The **Verdant Wrath** erupts. Gargantuan, furious, ancient. It cannot be killed. It can only be calmed.

### The Calming Begins

Mimbi uses her action each round to sing the old Feywild nursery rhyme. **DC 15 Charisma check.** Three successes needed for the first sequence.

While she sings, the Wrath fights — Vine Lash grapples, Eruption Slam knocks prone, legendary actions chip away at anyone trying to help.

### Complication — The Otyugh Arrives

**The first time the party suffers a bad round — a failed Charisma save from Mimbi, or any round that goes badly wrong for the table** — the ground nearby tears open. The disturbance from the Wrath's eruption has destabilised the earth further out than the portal itself.

The **Rift-Born Otyugh** claws up through the new rupture, **Rift Rage** active — advantage on its attacks, +2 damage for its first round. **2 Ghouls** and **2 Carrion Crawlers** come with it, drawn through by the same disturbance.

The fight is now two fronts at once: the Wrath at the portal, the Otyugh and its retinue spreading out from the new rupture point. Mimbi has to keep singing through all of it — every round she's not protected or covered is a round the calming stalls.

### Soren's Final Appearance

**On the third success against the Wrath** — regardless of what else is happening with the Otyugh — the Wrath is almost still. Head tilted. Listening.

Soren arrives. He has been hunting the crystals across the entire city and has finally tracked them here, to the one place he couldn't reach in time to stop. He is out of patience and nearly out of options, and what he finds is the exact moment of triumph he's spent the whole campaign chasing.

> *"Remarkable. The energy output alone—"*

He raises the gauntlet. Fires both remaining charges directly at the Wrath — trying to absorb and weaponise the very power that's finally being calmed. His instinct. His mandate. His fatal flaw. Even now, surrounded by chaos he caused, he cannot stop himself.

**The Wrath does not get absorbed. It turns. Soren is gone in one motion.** No final speech. No last stand. Just gone. The gauntlet hits the ground and goes dark.

### The Reset

A moment of silence — however brief, given the Otyugh may still be active. Then the Wrath is awake and furious again. Verdant Surge recharges on 4–6 instead of 5–6. Wild Pulse costs one legendary point instead of two.

**Three more successes needed.** If the Otyugh and its retinue are still alive, the party is now fighting two escalated threats simultaneously with Soren gone and no one left to blame but the monsters themselves.

### Six Successes — The Wrath Stills

It does not die. It settles. Dissipates back through the portal into the Feywild where it belongs.

If the Otyugh is still alive when this happens, it loses whatever residual rift energy was empowering it the moment the portal stabilises — Rift Rage and the surge that drove it through in the first place dissipate with the Wrath. It becomes a normal, mortal, exhausted creature, and the fight against it (if not already finished) becomes straightforward from this point.

The portal stabilises. The final crystal locks into place.

For a moment, the portal shows Mimbi's hometown — colour, life, home — before it closes.

---

## Closing the Campaign

This is the moment to let the table breathe. A few beats you may want to hit:

- **The gauntlet** lies dark on the ground. Five charges spent across the whole campaign. It will never work again. The party can take it — a relic of everything Soren built and lost.
- **Mimbi**, after this surge — likely the largest yet, given the scale of this final placement — is probably hurting badly. The Crystal Resonance Amulet's Conduit Shield absorbs what it can, but this is the moment to acknowledge what carrying this role cost her across the whole campaign.
- **Thrynn**, having provided the warding that got the crystals here safely, gets her redemption beat — her land healing, the bleaching reversing, the thing Soren took from her at the Reach answered by the thing she gave the party here.
- **The city** — Helios gone, the Library and AG battered but standing, the crystals returned, Soren gone. Valandir gets to breathe, properly this time, after a much harder fight than anyone expected when this started with a dock and a barge.

## Key Stats — Quick Reference

**Thrynn's Dwarven Warding**
Mutes magical detection on the crystals entirely until the final ritual begins. Cannot be replicated quickly — old craft, not a spell. Spent once, for this.

**Verdant Wrath**
AC 17 · HP 230 (cannot be killed — must be calmed) · Save DC 18 · Vine Lash ×2 +13 (grapple) · Eruption Slam +13 · Verdant Surge recharge 5–6 (4–6 after Soren reset) · Legendary 3/round (Root Burst 1pt / Wild Pulse 2pt, 1pt after reset / Reform 3pt)

**Rift-Born Otyugh** *(arrives mid-fight, triggered by a bad round)*
AC 16 · HP 160 · Atk +6 · Save DC 14 · Rift Rage (first round only: advantage, +2 dmg) · Legendary 3/round (Tentacle Lash 1pt / Rift Pulse 2pt / Devour 3pt) · Loses Rift Rage source and becomes mortal/ordinary once the Wrath stills

**Ghouls ×2**
AC 12 · HP 22 each · Claws +4 (DC10 Con or paralysed) · Bite +2 adv vs incapacitated

**Carrion Crawlers ×2**
AC 13 · HP 51 each · Tentacle +4 (DC13 Con or paralysed) · Spider Climb

**The Calm Mechanic**
DC 15 Charisma, Mimbi's action each round. A failed save (or any bad round) triggers the Otyugh's arrival. 3 successes → Soren arrives and is eaten by the Wrath. Reset. 3 more successes → Wrath stills, Otyugh loses its empowerment if still alive. 6 total successes to end the encounter.

**Soren — Gauntlet (Portal, Final)**
2 charges remaining, both fired at the Wrath in his final act. All 5 charges spent across the campaign. Gauntlet left behind, dark, inert.

**Mimbi — Crystal Surge (Portal)**
Largest surge of the campaign. Absorbed by the Crystal Resonance Amulet's Conduit Shield property where possible — narrate the cost regardless.
