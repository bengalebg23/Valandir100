# Session 16 — Opening Scene: The Main Square

---

## Context

The party leaves the Library after delivering the crystals to Thalira. It is early morning. They have had a short rest but no long rest.

---

## The City

As they move through Valandir the atmosphere has shifted entirely. Bells are ringing across the city. Word of Helios' death has spread overnight and people are out in the streets — not riot, not fear, but genuine relief spilling into celebration.

Merchants lean out of doorways to thank them. One pulls them aside:

> *"Seventy, eighty percent of everything I made — gone. Every month. For six years. Do you know what I can do now? I can hire someone. I can grow. My children might actually inherit something."*

It is not a grand speech. It is a man who cannot quite believe it yet.

---

## The Main Square

The square is packed. The Adventurers' Guild standard is flying. Lady Hollingsworth is at the front, addressing the crowd — the changing of the guard, her resignation from the AG, the beginning of something new for Valandir.

The party arrives. The crowd recognises them. Rapturous applause.

---

## Soren Interrupts

The applause cuts short.

Soren Mourne steps forward from the edge of the square. Unhurried. He is well-dressed, composed, holding a folded document. He does not raise his voice — he doesn't need to. The crowd goes quiet around him.

> *"Forgive the interruption. I have a legal matter that requires immediate attention."*

He presents the contract to the square — loud enough for all to hear:

> *"The crystals currently in the possession of these adventurers were extracted from beneath this city under a lawful commercial agreement with the controlling authority of the Valandir undermarket. That authority was Helios. The contract predates his death by three days and is therefore valid independent of his subsequent fate. The crystals are Velmoran Institute property. What these adventurers have done is theft — however nobly motivated."*

He lets that land. Then:

> *"Furthermore, four of my researchers were killed on that dockside. I have not yet decided whether to pursue that through the city magistrate. That decision may depend on how this matter is resolved."*

Calm. Reasonable. Holding a copy of the contract — he anticipated this moment.

---

## The Party Argues

They will throw several arguments at him. He has an answer to all of them.

**"Helios had no legitimate authority — the contract is void."**
> *"That is a matter for the courts. In the meantime, by law, the crystals remain mine."*

**"The crystals belong to the city."**
> *"The city sold the extraction rights. I have the paperwork."*

**"The Feywild consequences affect everyone."**
> *"A compelling moral argument. Irrelevant to contract law."*

He is not wrong. That is what makes him dangerous in this scene.

---

## Lady Hollingsworth

She steps forward. Takes the contract from Soren's hand. Examines it carefully, expression unreadable.

She turns to the party and explains — loud enough for the crowd — that if the contract is valid, it is legally binding and must be upheld.

She turns back to Soren.

She casts Burning Hands on the parchment.

Holds the ash for a moment.

Looks up at Soren pleasantly.

> *"I'm sorry — could you show me that contract again?"*

---

## Soren's Response

He looks at the ash. He looks at her. He does not react — not visibly.

He turns to the party. The crowd noise is returning around them. He speaks at normal volume, for everyone to hear:

> *"This isn't over."*

He leaves. The applause resumes around him.

---

## What This Scene Establishes

- Lady Hollingsworth is an ally — and the kind of ally who solves problems with fire
- Soren is patient, well-prepared, and does not need to win today
- The crowd and the guild are with the party here — but that protection ends at the city walls
- The party knows he is coming. He knows where the convergence points are.
